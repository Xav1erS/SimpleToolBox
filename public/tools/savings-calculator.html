What is Savings Calculator?

Savings Calculator projects the future value of a savings account or investment using compound interest. It accounts for an initial lump sum plus regular monthly contributions, with configurable compounding frequency. A year-by-year table shows exactly how your balance grows over time.

How to Use Savings Calculator

  1. Enter your current or initial balance.
  2. Set a monthly contribution amount (can be 0).
  3. Enter the expected annual interest rate.
  4. Choose a time period and compounding frequency.
  5. Read the future balance, total contributions, and interest earned.
  6. Click "Show Year-by-Year Growth" for the full table.

Frequently Asked Questions
How is compound interest calculated?

FV = PV × (1 + r/n)^(n×t) + PMT × [((1+r/n)^(n×t) − 1) / (r/n)], where PV is the starting balance, PMT is the monthly contribution, r is the annual rate, n is the compounding frequency, and t is the years.

What compound frequency should I choose?

Most savings accounts and CDs compound monthly or daily. Money market accounts often compound daily. Choose the frequency that matches your account type for the most accurate projection.

Does this account for inflation?

No. The calculator shows nominal returns. To estimate real purchasing power, subtract the expected inflation rate from your annual interest rate.

What is the difference between APR and APY?

APR is the stated rate before compounding. APY (Annual Percentage Yield) is the effective annual rate after compounding. For monthly compounding at 5% APR, the APY is about 5.12%.
