What is HEIC to JPG Converter?

HEIC to JPG Converter lets you convert HEIC and HEIF photos taken on iPhone or iPad to widely compatible JPEG or PNG format. Everything runs in your browser using the open-source heic2any library — your photos are never uploaded anywhere.

How to Convert HEIC to JPG

  1. Drop one or more .heic or .heif files onto the upload zone, or click to browse.
  2. Choose output format — JPEG (default) for photos, or PNG for lossless output.
  3. Adjust the quality slider if needed (JPEG only; default 92 is a good balance).
  4. Click Convert All and wait a moment for each file to decode.
  5. Download files individually or use Download All as ZIP for batch export.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is a HEIC file?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's default photo format since iOS 11. It uses the HEIF standard to store images at roughly half the file size of JPEG while maintaining comparable visual quality. Most non-Apple devices and older software cannot open HEIC files natively, which is why conversion to JPEG or PNG is often needed.

Are my photos uploaded to a server?

No. All conversion is performed locally in your browser using the heic2any JavaScript library. Your photos never leave your device and no data is sent to any server.

Can I convert multiple HEIC files at once?

Yes. You can select multiple files in the file picker or drag and drop several HEIC files at once onto the upload zone. Each file is converted individually with its own progress indicator, and you can download them one by one or all together as a ZIP archive.

Why does conversion take a moment?

HEIC decoding is computationally intensive compared to reading a standard JPEG. The heic2any library must fully decode the HEIF bitstream in JavaScript before re-encoding to JPEG or PNG. Large files or batch conversions may take a few seconds depending on your device speed.
