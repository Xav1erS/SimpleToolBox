What is Image Sharpener?

Image Sharpener uses an unsharp mask convolution to enhance edge detail and clarity in blurry or soft images. It runs entirely in your browser, so your photos are never uploaded anywhere.

How to Use Image Sharpener

  1. Upload an image by clicking the drop zone or dragging a file onto it.
  2. Adjust the Sharpness slider to increase or decrease edge enhancement.
  3. Click Download to save the sharpened image to your device.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is my image uploaded to a server?

No. All sharpening happens entirely in your browser using the Canvas API. Your image never leaves your device.

Which image formats are supported?

You can upload any format your browser supports, including JPG, PNG, WebP, and GIF. The output is downloaded as PNG (lossless) or JPEG depending on the original format.

Does sharpening cause quality loss?

Unsharp masking enhances edge contrast and does not reduce image resolution. However, very high sharpness values can introduce visible halos around edges, so moderate settings usually produce the best results.

What is an unsharp mask?

Unsharp mask is a classic sharpening technique. It subtracts a blurred version of the image from the original to amplify edge details. Despite the name, it makes images look sharper by increasing local contrast at edges.
