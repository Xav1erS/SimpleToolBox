What is Color Contrast Checker?

Calculates the WCAG relative luminance contrast ratio between any two colors. Provides instant pass/fail feedback for AA and AAA accessibility levels across text sizes and UI components.

How to Use Color Contrast Checker

  1. Enter or pick two colors (foreground and background).
  2. See the contrast ratio calculated instantly.
  3. View pass/fail for WCAG AA and AAA at normal and large text sizes.
  4. Try suggested color adjustments if the combination fails.

Background #FFFFFF (white) → Ratio: 4.48:1

Why Contrast Matters
  • Low-vision users — approximately 1 in 12 people have some degree of low vision; sufficient contrast makes text legible for them.
  • Outdoor / bright environments — glare and reflections reduce perceived contrast on screens by up to 50%.
  • Color blindness — about 8% of males have color vision deficiency; high luminance contrast ensures readability regardless of hue.
  • Legal compliance — WCAG AA is mandated under ADA (US), EN 301 549 (EU), and similar laws globally.
  • Better UX for everyone — high-contrast text reduces eye strain and improves reading speed for all users.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between AA and AAA contrast levels?

WCAG AA requires a 4.5:1 ratio for normal text and 3:1 for large text — the standard legal requirement. AAA requires 7:1 for normal text and 4.5:1 for large text, providing enhanced accessibility for users with severe low vision.

Does this check non-text contrast (icons, borders)?

Yes — WCAG 1.4.11 (Non-text Contrast) requires 3:1 for UI components and graphical elements. The AA UI Components result in the table covers this requirement.

My colors pass AA but fail AAA — do I need to fix them?

AAA compliance is not required for all content. WCAG AA is the standard legal requirement in most jurisdictions. Aim for AAA where possible, but AA is sufficient for legal compliance.

What counts as large text?

WCAG defines large text as 18pt (24px) or larger for regular weight, or 14pt (approximately 18.67px) or larger for bold text. Large text has a lower contrast requirement (3:1 for AA) because it is inherently easier to read.
