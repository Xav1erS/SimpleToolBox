Base58 is Base64 with visually ambiguous characters removed: 0 (zero), O (capital-o), I (capital-i), l (lowercase-L), +, and / are all excluded. This makes Base58 strings safe for manual copying and easy to read aloud. It is widely used for Bitcoin addresses, IPFS CIDs (v0), and other content-addressed identifiers. The three alphabets differ only in character ordering Рђћchoose the one that matches your target system.

What is Base58?

Base58 is a compact text encoding that removes confusing characters such as 0, O, I, and l. It is often used in cryptocurrency addresses, content identifiers, and systems where humans may need to read or retype the encoded value.

No Рђћthis is standard Base58 without checksum. Bitcoin's Base58Check adds a 4-byte SHA256d checksum.

Why not just use Base64?

Base58 omits ambiguous characters (0/O and I/l) that are identical in many fonts, reducing manual copy errors.
