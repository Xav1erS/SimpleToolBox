Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between camelCase and PascalCase?

camelCase starts with a lowercase letter and capitalises each subsequent word (e.g. helloWorld). PascalCase capitalises every word including the first (e.g. HelloWorld). camelCase is common in JavaScript variables; PascalCase is used for class names.

Does it handle special characters and numbers?

Yes. Numbers are preserved in their original position, and most punctuation is stripped when generating code-style cases like snake_case or kebab-case.

Can I convert multiple lines at once?

Yes. Paste multi-line text and every line is converted to the selected case format simultaneously.

Is my text stored or shared anywhere?

No. All processing happens locally in your browser. No data is sent to any server.
