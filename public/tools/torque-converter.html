What is a Torque Converter?

A torque converter translates rotational force measurements between unit systems. Newton-meters (N·m) is the SI unit of torque. Foot-pounds (ft·lb) and inch-pounds (in·lb) are used in the US for fastener specs and engine ratings. Kilogram-force meters (kgf·m) appear in older and some Asian engineering documents. Kilonewton-meters (kN·m) are used in structural engineering.

Frequently Asked Questions
How many ft·lb is 1 N·m?

1 N·m = 0.7376 ft·lb (foot-pounds).

How many in·lb is 1 N·m?

1 N·m = 8.851 in·lb (inch-pounds).

What is the difference between torque and force?

Force is a push or pull (measured in newtons). Torque is a rotational force — force applied at a distance from a pivot point. Torque = Force x Lever Arm length.

What is kgf·m used for?

Kilogram-force meter (kgf·m) is a legacy unit used in older engineering literature and some Asian countries. 1 kgf·m = 9.80665 N·m.
