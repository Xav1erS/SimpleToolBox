Base58 is Base64 with visually ambiguous characters removed: 0 (zero), O (capital-o), I (capital-i), l (lowercase-L), +, and / are all excluded. This makes Base58 strings safe for manual copying and easy to read aloud. It is widely used for Bitcoin addresses, IPFS CIDs (v0), and other content-addressed identifiers. The three alphabets differ only in character ordering — choose the one that matches your target system.

What is Base58 Encoder / Decoder?

Encodes data using the Base58 alphabet, which removes visually ambiguous characters (0, O, I, l) from Base64. Widely used in Bitcoin addresses, IPFS content identifiers, and other blockchain applications.

Base58 Use Cases
  • Bitcoin: wallet addresses use Base58Check (Base58 + 4-byte checksum)
  • IPFS: legacy CIDv0 content identifiers use Base58
  • Short URLs: compact ID encoding without ambiguous characters

Frequently Asked Questions
Is this Bitcoin's Base58Check?

No — this is standard Base58 without checksum. Bitcoin's Base58Check adds a 4-byte SHA256d checksum.

Why not just use Base64?

Base58 omits ambiguous characters (0/O and I/l) that are identical in many fonts, reducing manual copy errors.
