What is an Energy Converter?

An energy converter translates a quantity of work or heat between different unit systems. The joule (J) is the SI unit. The calorie (cal) and kilocalorie (kcal) measure heat and food energy. The watt-hour (Wh) and kilowatt-hour (kWh) measure electrical energy. The BTU (British Thermal Unit) is used in heating and cooling systems, especially in the US.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the difference between calories and kcal?

1 kcal (kilocalorie) = 1000 cal (small calories). Food labels always use kcal —the "Calorie" on packaging is actually a kilocalorie.

How many joules in 1 kWh?

1 kWh = 3,600,000 J = 3.6 MJ.

What is a BTU?

BTU (British Thermal Unit) is the energy needed to heat 1 pound of water by 1°F. 1 BTU = 1055.06 J.

How do I convert food calories to joules?

Multiply kcal by 4184. For example, 100 kcal = 418,400 J = 418.4 kJ.
