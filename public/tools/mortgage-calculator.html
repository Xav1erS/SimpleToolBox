What is Mortgage Calculator?

Mortgage Calculator computes the monthly principal and interest payment for a fixed-rate home loan using the standard amortization formula. It also shows total interest over the life of the loan, a full PITI estimate when you add property tax and insurance, and a year-by-year amortization schedule.

How to Use Mortgage Calculator

  1. Enter the home price and down payment.
  2. Set the annual interest rate and loan term.
  3. Optionally add monthly property tax and insurance amounts.
  4. Read the monthly payment, total interest, and total cost.
  5. Click "Show Amortization Schedule" for a year-by-year breakdown.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the formula for a mortgage payment?

Monthly payment M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1], where P is the loan amount (home price minus down payment), r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12), and n is the number of monthly payments (years × 12).

What is included in PITI?

PITI stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. The calculator shows both the P&I payment and the full PITI total when you add monthly property tax and home insurance amounts.

How much down payment do I need?

Conventional loans typically require 5–20% down. A 20% down payment avoids Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). FHA loans allow as little as 3.5% down.

Does the amortization schedule show early payoff?

The table shows the yearly schedule at the specified rate and term. To model early payoff, try different loan terms in the term dropdown.
