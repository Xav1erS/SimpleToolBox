What is a Calorie Burn Calculator?

A calorie burn calculator estimates the energy expended during physical activity using MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) values. MET values are published in the Compendium of Physical Activities, a research database covering hundreds of activities from sleeping to marathon running. The formula is simple: kcal = MET × weight in kg × duration in hours.

How to Use the Calorie Burn Calculator

  1. Enter your body weight and choose kg or lbs.
  2. Enter the duration of your activity in minutes.
  3. Browse activity categories (Cardio, Sports, Gym, Daily) and click an activity.
  4. The calorie estimate updates instantly with the MET value used.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is a MET value?

MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) measures the energy cost of an activity relative to sitting still (1 MET). Running at 8 km/h has a MET of 8, meaning it burns 8× more energy than sitting.

How accurate is the calorie estimate?

The formula gives a reasonable estimate, but actual calorie burn varies by age, sex, fitness level, and intensity. Use it as a guide, not a precise measurement.

What is the formula used?

Calories (kcal) = MET × body weight (kg) × duration (hours). This is the standard formula derived from the Compendium of Physical Activities.

Can I enter weight in pounds?

Yes. Toggle the unit button between kg and lbs — the tool converts automatically.
