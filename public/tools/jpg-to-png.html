What is a JPG to PNG Converter?

A JPG to PNG converter transforms JPEG images into PNG format in your browser. Unlike JPEG which uses lossy compression, PNG uses lossless compression — meaning every pixel is preserved exactly with no quality loss. PNG also supports transparency (alpha channel), which JPEG does not. This converter runs entirely in your browser; your images are never sent to any server.

How to Convert JPG to PNG

  1. Drop your JPEG or JPG image onto the preview area, or click to browse and select a file.
  2. The image is instantly converted to PNG format in your browser.
  3. Check the file size comparison in the left panel — PNG files are typically larger than JPEG.
  4. Click Download PNG to save the converted file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the PNG file larger than the original JPEG?

JPEG uses aggressive lossy compression that achieves very small file sizes by discarding some image data. PNG uses lossless compression, which preserves all data, resulting in larger files. This is a trade-off: PNG quality is higher, JPEG size is smaller.

Does converting JPG to PNG improve quality?

No. Any quality lost during the original JPEG compression cannot be recovered. However, saving as PNG prevents any further quality loss — your PNG will be a perfect lossless copy of the JPEG, no worse and no better.

Will the PNG have a transparent background?

Not automatically — JPEG images don't have transparency information. The resulting PNG will have the same opaque background as the original JPEG. To add transparency, use a background removal tool after converting.

Can I convert PNG back to JPG?

Yes. Use the PNG to JPG Converter to convert in the other direction. Note that converting to JPEG will apply lossy compression.
