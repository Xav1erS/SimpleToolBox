What is Sentence Counter?

Sentence Counter estimates how many sentences appear in a block of pasted text. It also shows supporting writing stats such as average words per sentence and paragraph count, which makes it useful for editing and readability checks.

How to Use Sentence Counter

  1. Paste the text you want to analyze into the input area.
  2. Review the sentence total, word count, average length, and paragraph count.
  3. Scan the per-sentence breakdown if you want to inspect long or short lines.
  4. Adjust your draft elsewhere and paste again to compare the new count.

Frequently Asked Questions
How does it split sentences?

It uses common sentence-ending punctuation such as periods, exclamation marks, and question marks as a practical browser-side estimate.

Can it handle multiple paragraphs?

Yes. It also estimates paragraph count based on blank-line paragraph breaks.

Is the count always perfect?

No automated sentence parser is perfect. Abbreviations and unusual punctuation can affect the estimate, but it works well for most normal prose.

Is the text uploaded?

No. The analysis stays in your browser.
