What is URL Extractor?

URL Extractor pulls links out of pasted text and turns them into a clean one-per-line list. It is useful for notes, logs, scraped content, markdown, and any text where links are mixed with other content.

How to Use URL Extractor

  1. Paste text that contains one or more URLs.
  2. Choose whether to remove duplicates, sort the links, or normalize www links.
  3. Review the extracted URL list and counters instantly.
  4. Copy the cleaned links into your audit, spreadsheet, or next tool.

Frequently Asked Questions
Does it extract plain www.example.com links?

Yes. Links starting with www. are matched, and you can normalize them to https:// URLs with one option.

Will it remove trailing commas or periods?

Yes. Common trailing punctuation is stripped so copied links are cleaner.

Can I sort and deduplicate the result?

Yes. Both options are enabled by default, and you can turn them off if you want the original order preserved.

Is the text sent to a server?

No. All extraction happens locally in your browser.
