What Instagram Image Sizes Should I Use?

Instagram displays images at specific aspect ratios and crops anything outside those ratios. Using the correct dimensions ensures your photo is displayed exactly as intended without unexpected cropping. The recommended sizes are: Feed Square: 1080×1080, Feed Portrait: 1080×1350 (highest reach), Feed Landscape: 1080×566, Story/Reels: 1080×1920. This tool resizes your image to any of these dimensions directly in your browser — no upload required.

How to Resize an Image for Instagram

  1. Drop your image onto the preview area, or click to browse and select a file.
  2. Select the Instagram format: Feed Square, Portrait, Landscape, Story/Reels, or Profile Photo.
  3. Choose a fit mode: Cover Crop (fills the frame, crops edges) or Letterbox (fits inside, adds padding).
  4. For Letterbox mode, choose a background color for the padding areas.
  5. Click Download to save the resized image.

Instagram Format Reference
  • Feed Square (1080×1080) — Classic format, works for any content. Ratio 1:1.
  • Feed Portrait (1080×1350) — Takes up the most vertical space in the feed. Highest reach per post. Ratio 4:5.
  • Feed Landscape (1080×566) — Wider than square. Best for panoramic shots. Ratio ~1.91:1.
  • Story / Reels (1080×1920) — Full-screen vertical format. Ratio 9:16.
  • Profile Photo (400×400) — Displayed as a circle; center-focused content works best.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Instagram image size for reach?

Portrait (1080×1350) takes up the most vertical space in the Instagram feed, which typically leads to better engagement and reach. If your image is already square or landscape, Feed Square (1080×1080) is fine.

What is the difference between Cover Crop and Letterbox?

Cover Crop scales the image to fill the entire target frame, cropping any overflow from the edges. The center of the image is preserved. Letterbox scales the image to fit entirely within the target frame, adding colored padding bars on the sides or top/bottom (like a movie letterbox).

Will Instagram compress my image after uploading?

Yes. Instagram re-compresses uploaded images. To minimize quality loss, upload JPEG at high quality (85–100%) and at the exact pixel dimensions. Uploading an oversized image forces Instagram to scale it down, which adds another compression step.

Can I use this tool for other social media platforms?

Yes. The Cover Crop and Letterbox fit modes work for any target dimensions. For custom sizes use the Image Resizer tool instead.
